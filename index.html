The Dubai City

I like Dubai because of the sheer scale on which the city is built. It is completely futuristic but with a warm, old-world ethos.

Here are the most beautiful things about Dubai

  • Burj Khalifa

The Burj Khalifa (previously known as Burj Dubai prior to inauguration) is a megatall skyscraper in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. Designed by Skidmore, Owings & Merrill, it is the world's tallest structure, with a total height of 829.8 m (2,722 ft, or just over half a mile) and a roof height (excluding the antenna, but including a 242.6 m spire) of 828 m (2,717 ft). It has also been the tallest building in the world since its topping out in 2009, surpassing Taipei 101, which had held the record since 2004.

  • Dubai Marina

Dubai Marina is a vibrant, man-made waterfront district famous for its glittering skyline, towering residential skyscrapers, and bustling 3.5 km canal. Often called "New Dubai," it blends luxurious living with world-class dining, shopping, and nautical leisure, serving as a premier destination for tourists and expatriates alike
Recognized as one of the coolest neighborhoods in the world, the area transitions from a relaxing daytime community into a bustling hub of nightlife as the sun goes down. The streets are known for gleaming supercars, while the waterways are filled with yachts, traditional dhows, and local water taxis.

  • Palm Jumeirah

The Palm Jumeirah is a world-famous, palm-tree-shaped artificial archipelago in Dubai. Built entirely from reclaimed land, it added 56 kilometers of beachfront to the city’s coastline. The island serves as a major hub for luxury resorts, high-end dining, and exclusive residential properties
The island is divided into three mainsections:
The Trunk: The main entrance and "stem" of the palm. It is connected to the Dubai mainland by a 1.4 km bridge and features mid-to-high-rise apartments, shopping centers (such as Nakheel Mall), and parks.
The Fronds: Seventeen branches radiating outward, strictly reserved for ultra-luxury beachfront villas and mansions.
The Outer Crescent: A protective 11-kilometer breakwater that houses some of Dubai’s most famous five-star resorts, including Atlantis, The Palm and Atlantis The Royal.

  • Museum of Future

The Museum of the Future is an iconic 7-story architectural marvel in Dubai that explores scientific and technological innovation. Shaped like a glowing, calligraphy-engraved torus, it immerses visitors in interactive experiences set in the year 2071, focusing on climate change, space exploration, and human wellness.
Standing 77 meters (225 feet) tall, the torus-shaped structure represents humanity, while the open, elliptical center represents the unknown future.
Its exterior comprises 1,024 stainless steel panels manufactured by robots and is adorned with an Arabic poem written by Dubai's ruler, Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum.
Designed by Killa Design and engineered by Buro Happold, it relies on 4,000 MWh of solar energy annually.
